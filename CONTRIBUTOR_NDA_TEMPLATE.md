### 3.2 Exclusions from Confidential Information

Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

(a) Is or becomes publicly available through no breach of this Agreement;

(c) Is independently developed by Receiving Party without use of Confidential Information;
(d) Is required to be disclosed by law or court order (with prior notice if legally possible);
(e) Is general knowledge, skills, or experience retained in unaided memory after termination.

## 4. OBLIGATIONS OF RECEIVING PARTY

### 4.1 Duty of Confidentiality

Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) **Maintain Secrecy**: Keep all Confidential Information strictly confidential
(b) **Limit Access**: Disclose only to employees/contractors with a need-to-know
(c) **Protect Information**: Use same degree of care as protecting own confidential information (minimum reasonable care)
(d) **No Unauthorized Use**: Use Confidential Information solely for permitted contribution purposes
(e) **No Copying**: Do not copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as necessary for authorized work
(f) **No Transfer**: Do not transfer, assign, or license Confidential Information to any third party

### 4.2 Access Controls

Receiving Party must:

(a) Store Confidential Information securely (encrypted if digital)
(b) Limit physical access to authorized personnel only
(c) Use secure communication channels (HTTPS, VPN, encrypted email)
(d) Lock computers when away from desk
(e) Use strong passwords and multi-factor authentication
(f) Not access information outside their contribution scope
(g) Report any unauthorized access or loss immediately

### 4.3 Data Handling

Receiving Party must:

(a) Never copy Confidential Information to personal devices without explicit written permission
(b) Never share Confidential Information on public networks or unsecured devices
(c) Never discuss Confidential Information in public places or with unauthorized persons
(d) Never upload to cloud services without explicit written permission
(e) Understand that PII (Personally Identifiable Information) requires extra protection
(f) Comply with all applicable data protection laws (GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA, etc.)

### 4.4 Return of Information

Upon termination or request, Receiving Party must:

(a) Return all physical copies of Confidential Information
(b) Delete all digital copies from personal devices
(c) Clear all temporary storage and cache
(d) Confirm in writing that all materials have been returned/destroyed
(e) Not retain any copies except as required by law

## 7. SECURITY & COMPLIANCE

### 7.1 Security Standards

Receiving Party agrees to:

(a) Use strong passwords (minimum 16 characters, mixed case, numbers, symbols)
(b) Enable two-factor authentication on all accounts
(c) Keep systems and software updated and patched
(d) Use antivirus and anti-malware software
(e) Never use public WiFi without VPN
(f) Report security incidents immediately

### 7.2 Compliance Requirements

Receiving Party must comply with:

(a) **Data Protection**: GDPR, HIPAA, CCPA, and local privacy laws
(b) **Security Standards**: FIPS 140-2, NIST 800-53 compliance
(c) **Encryption**: Use AES-256 or better for sensitive data
(f) **Code Quality**: Follow project coding standards and security guidelines
(g) **Vulnerability**: Never knowingly introduce vulnerabilities

### 7.3 Bug/Vulnerability Disclosure

If Receiving Party discovers a security issue:

(a) Do NOT publicly disclose
(b) Contact Company security team immediately
(c) Provide detailed reproduction steps
(d) Allow reasonable time for fix (typically 30-90 days)
(e) Do NOT exploit or verify in production systems
(f) May be eligible for bug bounty (if applicable)

**Security Contact**: security@haven.local
