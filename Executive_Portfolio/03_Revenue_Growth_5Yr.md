## Growth Strategy

HAVEN scales through a **regional → statewide → national** expansion model anchored in government procurement cycles and measurable ROI evidence from each prior deployment tier.

As a universal civic navigation platform, HAVEN's addressable market extends far beyond social services — any county, municipality, court system, DMV office, workforce board, veterans office, or government-adjacent organization is a prospective deployment. Every government entity that currently requires citizens to navigate bureaucracy manually is a potential customer.

| Phase | Timeline | Geography | Driver |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Pilot | 2025 Q1–Q2 | Santa Clara County + Bay Area | Proof of concept; KPI evidence |
| Regional | 2025 Q3–2026 Q1 | Bay Area + major CA cities | Referral from pilot; CMAS procurement |
| Statewide | 2026 | All 58 CA counties | CA DHSS partnership; state contract |
| Multi-state | 2027 | TX, NY, IL, FL | Federal HUD / HHS referrals |
| National | 2028–2029 | Top 100 US metros | GSA schedule; federal contract vehicle |

## Key Revenue Assumptions

- **Average contract value (ACV)**: $120K–240K/year (Professional tier)
- **Net revenue retention (NRR)**: 115%+ (expansion within existing accounts)
- **Sales cycle**: 3–6 months (government procurement)
- **Churn**: <5% (switching cost is high; residents + caseworkers depend on the platform)
- **CAC payback period**: 8–12 months
- **LTV:CAC ratio**: 10:1+ at scale
